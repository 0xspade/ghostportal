Responsible Disclosure Policy

{{ platform_name }} · Operated by {{ operator_name }}

Our Commitment

We believe that responsible security research benefits everyone. If you have discovered a security vulnerability in our systems, we encourage you to disclose it to us responsibly so that we can fix it and protect our users.

Disclosure Timeline

  • We will acknowledge your report within 72 hours of submission.
  • We will provide a status update within 7 days.
  • We aim to resolve critical vulnerabilities within 30 days and high-severity issues within 60 days.
  • We will notify you when the vulnerability has been resolved.

Scope

In Scope:

  • Security vulnerabilities in systems operated by {{ operator_name }}
  • Authentication bypasses
  • Remote code execution
  • SQL injection
  • Cross-site scripting (XSS)
  • Privilege escalation
  • Sensitive data exposure

Out of Scope:

  • Social engineering attacks
  • Physical attacks
  • Denial of service attacks
  • Spam or phishing campaigns
  • Automated scanning without prior authorization

Safe Harbor

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

  • Disclose vulnerabilities in good faith following this policy
  • Do not access, modify, or delete production data
  • Do not perform actions that degrade service availability
  • Report vulnerabilities promptly and do not disclose them publicly before resolution

What We Ask of You

  • Give us reasonable time to respond and fix the issue before public disclosure
  • Do not exploit the vulnerability beyond what is necessary to demonstrate it
  • Do not access other users' data
  • Keep the vulnerability confidential until we provide a fix

How to Report

Submit vulnerability reports to: {{ operator_email }}

Please include:

  • Description of the vulnerability
  • Steps to reproduce
  • Proof of concept (if applicable)
  • Potential impact

Researcher Recognition

We are grateful for the security community's efforts. Researchers who responsibly disclose vulnerabilities may be credited in our security advisories with their consent.
